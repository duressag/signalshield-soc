Why Reusing Passwords Is Dangerous

Published in Security Best Practices

Password reuse is one of the most common cybersecurity risks today. When you use the same password across multiple services, a breach at a single low-security website can compromise your entire online identity.

How Credential Stuffing Works

Automated bots take leaked username and password combinations from data breaches and systematically test them across hundreds of popular platforms (email, banking, social media).

Key Action Steps

  • Use a Password Manager: Generate and store unique, complex passwords for every account.
  • Enable Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA): Require a second layer of verification even if your password leaks.
  • Check for Breaches: Use services like Have I Been Pwned to monitor compromised accounts.
